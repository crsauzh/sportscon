Call for Papers and Projects

The conference will host academic research and data driven journalism projects. Please make sure to submit your abstract for your preferred track. You can download our Call for Papers below.

Academic Track

Download Call for Papaers - Academic Track

The conference is open to all contributions that use data science methods in the area of sports research. To ensure interdisciplinary knowledge exchange, we strongly encourage submissions from diverse fields (e.g., data science, econometrics, communication and media research, psychology, network analytics, information science, etc.).


Research topics may include but are not limited to:

  • Applied data science / analytics / econometrics in professional and/or leisure sports including eSports
  • Measuring devices (e.g., mobile apps, smart appliances) and sports participation and/or health outcomes
  • Fan behavior on social media
  • Media usage in sports (e.g., digital media)
  • Social Network Analysis in sports (e.g., agent-player networks, player networks, fan networks, organizational networks, etc.)
  • Research on data driven journalism in the area of sports

Please make sure to state your data, methods, and results in your abstract (max. 600 words). Completed research or research in progress is admissible. Please note that manuscripts which are already accepted for publication cannot be submitted. The conference will be held in English.

Please make sure to submit your abstracts before May 31, 2018, via this form. You will be redirected to a Google Forms page.

Data Driven Journalism Track

Download Call for Papaers - Data Driven Journalism Track

The conference is open to all contributions that use data science and analytical methods, with a specific focus on data driven journalism in sports. Although we will focus on sports, more general contributions on research, analysis and visualization methods that are suitable for use in sports data journalism are also welcome.


Project examples:

  • The Tennis Racket by Heidi Blake and John Templon
  • Lionel Messi is Impossible by Benjamin Morris
  • Biased Swiss Judges by Barnaby Skinner, Simone Rau, and Salome Müller
  • Who will win Euro 2016 by NZZ Storytelling
  • Die Hochburgen der Schweizer Fussballfans by Timo Grossenbacher, Mario Stäuble, Patrice Siegrist, and Julian Schmidli

Please make sure to state your data, methods, and results in your abstract (max. 600 words). Completed projects or work in progress is admissible. You are welcome to provide us with the web links (e.g., GitHub, Medium, Blog, Website, etc.) to your article/project.

Please make sure to submit your abstracts before May 31, 2018, via this form. You will be redirected to a Google Forms page.
